The Kid Dynamite

Mike Tyson, born on June 30, 1966, in Brooklyn, New York, is a former professional boxer and one of the most celebrated figures in the history of the sport. Rising from a challenging background, Tyson became the youngest heavyweight champion at the age of 20, capturing the WBC title in 1986. Known for his ferocious style and incredible knockout power, Tyson dominated the boxing world, becoming the undisputed heavyweight champion in 1987.

Despite personal challenges, legal troubles, and a period of incarceration, Tyson made a remarkable comeback in the mid-'90s. His boxing legacy is marked by an unparalleled combination of skill and intimidation in the ring. Beyond his athletic prowess, Tyson's life has been a story of resilience and redemption. This tribute page honors Mike Tyson's impact on the sport of boxing, acknowledging his undeniable talent, historic achievements, and enduring influence.

Mentor - Cus D'Amato

January 17, 1908 - November 4, 1985

A legendary boxing trainer and manager who worked with several successful fighters. Recognized for developing the "Peekaboo" style of boxing, emphasizing defensive techniques and aggressive head movement. Became Tyson's legal guardian and mentor after Tyson's troubled childhood. Played a crucial role in Tyson's early career, guiding him to become the youngest heavyweight champion.

Cus D'Amato assumed the role of a paternal figure and guide for Tyson, offering support both within and beyond the boxing arena. D'Amato's training principles and mentorship played a pivotal role in Tyson's achievements. The "Peekaboo" style, originated by D'Amato, became closely associated with Tyson's early supremacy in the heavyweight division. Tyson frequently acknowledged D'Amato for molding his mindset and instilling a sense of discipline. The bond between Mike Tyson and Cus D'Amato stands as a noteworthy chapter in boxing history, exemplifying the influence of mentorship and the transformative impact of a positive guiding force on the life of a young athlete.
